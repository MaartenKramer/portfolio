About me

Game Designer & Game Developer

Hi! my name is Maarten and I'm currently studying Game Development at the HKU in Utrecht. I've always had a strong passion for all things creative. Whether it's games, music, writing, or making websites like this one! I usually spend my free time gaming, listening to music, or DM'ing my Dungeons and Dragons campaign. I love personal expression and am always looking for environments that allow me to do so.

I'm a multifaceted designer with a focus on narratives, and I like taking that mindset with me while I work. Through interesting hooks and concepts, and iterations that build well on those elements, I am able to make fun and unique experiences. With the games I make I also like to tackle different genres or themes. So whether it's a dark haunted basement or a secret society of trash worshipping rats, if the debugging isn't too annoying, I'm having a good time!

I'm always bursting with different ideas and find myself easily getting inspired by different media and the weird and wonderful people around me.

Notable Projects

REMEMBRANCE

YOU SEE THEM, THEY SEE YOU

Remembrance is a reimagining of my first Unity game, The Maze, a horror stealth game. I wanted to build and expand on the horror aspects, give context to your actions, and enhance the visuals. I introduced a storyline and monster voice acting to make a more immersive experience. The monster now becomes more perceptive if the player moves or uses a flashlight and it now sports a disturbing new look.

C# | Audacity | 2025

THE RAT GAME

A MYSTICAL TRASH UTOPIA

Through a UN-organized games event on sustainable cities, my team and I began working on a cutesy rat game about a society in a trash can. I managed to create a lot of wonder by making their world very small and adding mystique to the world outside their trashcan. I took inspiration from old legends to make this world believable. I ended up contributing a lot as the team lead and the writer to our game, and we ended up with something truly special.

Role: Team Lead, Creative Lead, Co-Developer

C# | FMOD, Excel, Draw.io | 2025

Download

BURNING MEMORY

TRAUMA & ACCEPTANCE

As part of a seminar we were asked to make a game about coping. I ended up sharing with my team about my experience with losing my dad's home to a housefire and the indirect guilt I felt in not being there to "fix it". We ended up using this trauma as a jumping off point for a timeloop game where a character slowly has to come to terms with being unable to change the past, but being able to shape the future.

Role: Creative Lead, Co-Developer

C# | Audacity | 2025

Download

CAT'S TALE

A STORY COME TO LIFE

My team and I were asked to develop a game with a real life component to it. We decided on making a story book with an Arduino portal to load in characters and items into a game version of the story. I was in charge of developing the Arduino connectivity and building puzzle and stealth systems. During A/B testing, we noticed that players struggled more with the easy puzzle than the hard one. It was a great lesson in how player assumptions can shape the experience.

Role: Systems Developer, Mechanics Designer, Audio Implementation

C# | Audacity | 2024

Download

KOMMANDER

A KEEN EYE CAN SAVE LIVES

When starting at the HKU, they asked us to form a game concept around a word we had thought up prior. I had "Analyze". I thought a game where you analyze limited information in a dangerous situation would be engaging. So I developed KOMMANDER where you would guide a platoon of soldiers through enemy territory, but all in a text adventure form. You were also given one-time-use special units, which you had to deploy strategically.

HTML, CSS, JS | GIMP, Audacity | 2023
